About Me

In short: I am a Scientific Researcher, Data Analyst, Computational Programmer and Science Educator.

I am a Post-Doctoral Reseacher in Observational Cosmology. My primary research interest is to study the properties of the large scale structure of our Universe using cosmological simulations. I secured the title of PhD in Physics from University of Roma Tre in March 2020.

My research work is chiefly computational and therefore I do a lot of programming mainly in C++ and Python. I have 6+ years of experience in scientific programming, data analysis and data visualisation. I have developed several coding algorithms and pipelines in computational cosmology (which you can access via GitHub if you are interested!). I am currently acquiring my skills in Machine Learning, more specifically in developing Deep Learning Neural Networks in Natural Language Processing .

I speak English, German, Italian, Marathi and Hindi. Once every year, I deliver seminars on basics of Astronomy and Astrophysics and organise amateur astronomy workshops for undergraduate and high school children. You will also discover my artist ventures if you scroll down. See you there!

“Science is more than a body of knowledge; it is a way of thinking; it is an unending process. There is no single ultimate truth to be achieved, after which all the scientists can retire.” - Carl Sagan

I believe that curiosity, experimentation and adventure are the strongest human virtues that have led to phenomenal discoveries in science. According to me the most important professional qualities that I possess are:

  • Self-learning complex scientific concepts and simplifying them
  • Strategizing computational methods to understand and solve problems
  • Presenting my work in cleaner and simpler manner
  • Independently manage and channelise responsibilities
  • Efficiently drive collaborative research and team work

The motivation to study physics sparked in me when I coordinated astronomy event at my school. I was perplexed about the origins of planets and stars in the Universe. At the same time, I was very enthusiastic about mountaineering and adventure sports in nature. Initially I participated but soon I became co-ordinator of rappelling and hiking events in Western Ghats. I stared at the Milky Way during camping in the wild. In the realm of nature, often I asked questions about the Nature and its reality, the Universe as a whole and our purpose in this life. Soon enough I opted the path of seeking answers to these questions as my career....
